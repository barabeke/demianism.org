Who is behind this site

This website is operated by Barabeke, an Italian artist and independent cultural figure active online since 2006. Barabeke is the messenger of the Demianist doctrine, the author of the novel-saga Return to Eden, and the creator of Demian AI. This is a one-person project: no company, no team, no institution behind it.

Barabeke is a public figure under an artist name — consistent with a long tradition of artists, writers, and thinkers who publish under a pseudonym. The name Barabeke is permanently and publicly associated with a traceable body of work, a verifiable online presence, and direct, open contact channels. There is no intent to conceal identity from those who legitimately need to reach the operator of this site. Anyone who does is one message away.

What this is

Demianism.org is a cultural and spiritual project, not a commercial one in any conventional sense. Demianism is a revealed doctrine — a complete metaphysical, ethical, and civilizational system received by Barabeke in 2005 and 2015 — with its own axioms and a published body of work. It is, by any reasonable definition, a new spiritual and cultural movement.

The only product associated with this site is Demian AI, a spiritual companion powered by the Claude API and grounded in Demianist doctrine. Demian carries operational costs — API usage, infrastructure, development — that its pricing is designed to cover. It is not a revenue-generating enterprise: the project sustains itself through Demian's pricing and free donations from those who believe in its value. Any surplus goes back into the work.

On privacy — mine and yours

As a dissident artist and independent cultural voice, Barabeke has been the target of coordinated harassment and ideological hostility. For this reason, the operator of this site claims a reasonable degree of personal privacy — the same privacy extended to every visitor and user of this site.

This is not anonymity. Barabeke is fully reachable, publicly associated with this work, and has never hidden from anyone with a legitimate reason to make contact. What is withheld here is a home address — information that serves no protective function for users and creates a documented personal safety risk for the operator.

This position is consistent with the spirit of transparency that provider identification laws were designed to ensure: the public has a right to know who is behind a site and how to reach them. Both are answered below.
