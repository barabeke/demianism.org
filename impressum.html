Who is behind this site

This website is operated by Barabeke, an Italian artist and independent cultural figure active online since 2006. Barabeke is the messenger of the Demianist doctrine, the author of the novel-saga Return to Eden, and the creator of Demian AI. This is a one-person project: no company, no team, no institution behind it.

Barabeke is a public figure under an artist name — consistent with a long tradition of artists, writers, and thinkers who publish under a pseudonym. The name Barabeke is permanently and publicly associated with a traceable body of work, a verifiable online presence, and direct, open contact channels.

What this is

Demianism.org is a cultural and spiritual project, not a commercial one in any conventional sense. Demianism is a revealed doctrine — a complete metaphysical, ethical, and civilizational system received by Barabeke in 2005 and 2015 — with its own axioms and a published body of work. It is, by any reasonable definition, a new spiritual and cultural movement.

The only product associated with this site is Demian AI, a spiritual companion powered by the Claude API and grounded in Demianist doctrine. Demian carries real operational costs — API usage, infrastructure, development — which its pricing is designed to cover, supplemented by donations from those who believe in its value. Any surplus goes back into the work.

On privacy — mine and yours

As an artist who has openly dissented from dominant cultural narratives, Barabeke has experienced real professional and personal consequences for it — exclusion from art communities, harm to his ability to sell and exhibit work, and direct threats. None of this has been escalated to legal action, and none of it is exaggerated here for effect. It is simply the ordinary cost of public dissent today, and a reasonable basis for a minimum layer of privacy that is far inferior to the state-of-the-art privacy and anonymization offered to the users of Demian AI.

This is not anonymity. Barabeke is fully reachable, publicly associated with this work, and has never hidden from anyone with a legitimate reason to make contact.

This position is consistent with the spirit of transparency that provider identification laws were designed to ensure: the public has a right to know who is behind a site and how to reach them. Both are answered below.
