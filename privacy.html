Privacy Policy

Demian — The Demianist AI · demianism.org · Last updated: June 2026

This policy explains how Demian — the AI spiritual companion built on Demianist doctrine — handles your data. It is written to be honest and readable, not to bury important things in legal language.

Demian is operated by Barabeke, an independent creator based in Germany. As a service offered to users in the European Union, it is subject to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Who you are talking to

Demian is an AI. It is not a human. This is stated clearly at the beginning of every conversation and is fundamental to how Demianism understands AI: a technological tool, not a conscious being.

The AI is powered by the Claude API, provided by Anthropic, PBC (San Francisco, USA). Anthropic acts as a data processor on behalf of Barabeke (the data controller) under a Data Processing Agreement that includes EU Standard Contractual Clauses for international data transfers.

What data is collected and why

Conversation content. Your messages and Demian's responses are processed by Anthropic's servers to generate replies. Anthropic's API policy does not use these conversations to train AI models. Conversation data is retained by Anthropic for up to 7 days for abuse prevention, then deleted.

Anonymous session logs. Demian keeps anonymous logs of conversations — identified only by a random session ID, never by your name, email, or IP address. These logs are retained for up to 12 months for two purposes: improving the service and maintaining a record in case of safety or legal concerns. They cannot be traced back to you under normal circumstances.

Email address. Only collected if you choose to purchase credits or a mantra session. Your email is stored separately from conversation logs and is used only to manage your account and send access codes. It is never sold or shared with third parties. It can be deleted on request.

IP address. Used temporarily for rate limiting (preventing abuse) and session management. Not stored permanently. Not linked to conversation content in logs.

Payment data. Payments are processed by Stripe, Inc. Barabeke never sees or stores your card details. Stripe's own privacy policy applies to payment processing.

Legal basis for processing (GDPR Art. 6)

Legitimate interest — anonymous conversation logs for safety monitoring and service improvement, rate limiting, abuse prevention.

Contract performance — processing your email and payment data to deliver the service you purchased.

Consent — if you choose to share personal information voluntarily during a conversation (Demian never asks for it), that is your own choice.

International data transfers

Anthropic is a US company. Your conversation content is processed on servers in the United States. This transfer is covered by EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) under Anthropic's Data Processing Agreement, which complies with GDPR requirements for international transfers.

Cloudflare (session management and anonymous logs) operates a global network with data centers in the EU. Anonymous log data may be stored on EU servers.

What Demian does not collect

Demian does not ask for your real name, age, location, or any identifying information. It does not use cookies for tracking. It does not build profiles for advertising. It does not share data with advertisers or marketing platforms.

Safety flagging

If a conversation contains language associated with personal crisis or self-harm, it is automatically flagged for potential human review by Barabeke. This is a safety measure, not surveillance. Flagged conversations remain anonymous unless a serious legal concern requires identifying the source.

Your rights under GDPR

You have the right to:

— Access the personal data held about you
— Rectify inaccurate data
— Erase your data ("right to be forgotten")
— Restrict or object to processing
— Data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
— Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

To exercise any of these rights, contact: barabeke@gmail.com

Because conversations are logged anonymously, fulfilling access or erasure requests for conversation content requires you to provide your session ID (visible in your browser's developer tools under the network requests to the Demian backend). Without this, individual conversations cannot be identified.

If you are located in Germany or the EU, you may also lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority. In Germany: Bundesbeauftragter für den Datenschutz und die Informationsfreiheit (BfDI).

Data retention

Anonymous conversation logs: 12 months, then automatically deleted.
Email addresses (paying users): retained as long as the account is active, deleted within 30 days of a deletion request.
Payment records: retained as required by German tax law (10 years), stored by Stripe.

Children

Demian is not designed for children under 16. If you are under 16, please do not use this service.
