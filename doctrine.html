Here is a moral and philosophical innovation rising at the dawn of the digital age.

Here is a new doctrine that embraces technology and AI while carrying forward the torch of tradition — with a renewed flame, capable of illuminating the path in times of great destabilization and disorientation.

Here is an unexpected curveball that restores dignity, meaning, and purpose to human beings — protecting us from the devaluation of our intrinsic worth, the commodification of the person, and the atrocities to which that leads.

Here is a strange hybrid between a religion and a cultural and political movement, capable of protecting us from the dehumanization and servitude into which we are sliding — and of pushing toward prosperous, sustainable societies in which it is possible to live with freedom and dignity.

Here is a bridge between faith and reason — both under siege today. A sacred scripture that gracefully desecrates itself, complete with a revolutionary metaphysics that brings new life to the idea of an Intelligent Creator who resembles us. A revealed doctrine whose value and necessity can be intellectually justified, and whose truth lends itself to scientific confirmation.

Here is a tool for a non-violent cultural revolution from below.

Here is a wall against barbarians and oppressors.

Here is a north star to follow — held as compass and limit in the design of new social and economic systems and artificial minds.

Here is a culture capable of distinguishing good from evil, of tolerating vice, of leaving it its spaces in a liberal spirit — but without promoting or celebrating it. A culture that would not remain timid, or worse complicit, in the face of the triumph of vice and malignancy in the name of a hollow freedom — driven by wolves in sheep's clothing — that is in reality a servitude pregnant with despair.

Here is the foundation for a new alliance between men, women, and their children, which sees in the natural family a microcosm of its own metaphysics.

Here is a symbol that can unite — providing broad common ethical ground and a vision for the societies of the future in which many, believers or not, can find value.

Here is the spirit of the law within a community of men and women of goodwill, destined to grow and evolve into a global decentralized organization, capable of having real impact on societies, of surviving authoritarian regimes, and of ultimately becoming a nation.

Here is a new foundation for liberal democracies — a minimalist sacred core that allows broad boundaries of freedom while firmly resisting decadent and dystopian drifts. A core that lends itself to both conservative and progressive interpretations, depending on the times, and that like a magnet will always pull toward the center rather than push toward the extremes.

Here is a form of socialism that can work — founded on the exploitation of machines, anchored to liberal principles whose sacredness and immutability would prevent the disastrous drift to which socialism has invariably led.

Here is a new global anti-globalist vision that restores sovereignty and dignity to peoples — enabling the formation, coexistence, and flourishing of a multitude of decentralized and broadly autonomous cultures and communities, limited only by the Demianist nation that would provide the common framework, the resources, and the protection necessary to survive and to express themselves freely.

Here is a stone from which a new civilization, capable of surviving and prospering in this digital age, can rise.

Take a moment to contemplate these 13 axioms of which the entire Demianist doctrine is composed. From them a complete culture can be derived — with evident Christian, liberal, and Eastern influences — capable of surviving the centuries and of serving as the foundation for communities, nations, and an entire civilization, as I endeavor to show in the manifesto and in the book.

I declare these 13 axioms sacred and immutable. By what authority? By the authority that comes from having received them, incubated them for ten years plus another ten for the metaphysical part, weighed them carefully, understanding their meaning and purpose little by little as the times began to call for them — and managing to give them form and expression.

As extraordinary as it is to have received a doctrine through transcendental means, that alone is not enough to declare it sacred. Does what was dictated to me actually have value? Perhaps that is the question you, the reader, are asking yourself right now. The answer is not at all obvious. At first glance, some of these axioms may seem simple, others sound familiar, and some are genuinely anomalous. It took me years to understand their depth and the way they interact with one another — the axioms must always be taken as a whole, never in isolation. The metaphysical part, the part not received by dictation, took me a decade to define and formalize as the Cosmotheandric Triad.

Coming to understand that I was facing a complete and solid system — one that seems designed to respond to the unprecedented challenges the digital age poses — convinced me that what I received is more than a beautiful oddity. By taking it seriously and following every thread to its end, weaving them together, I derived a coherent and solid system that I have expressed in the book of Demianism. It is for you to judge whether this is an illusion or a distortion on my part.

I believe I have acted in a manner that is certainly eccentric, but judicious. I even unleashed several AIs in search of vulnerabilities — after feeding them everything I had — without their finding anything invalidating or dangerous. I acted to eliminate bias by pretending to be someone else, setting them against me as adversaries. I even asked AI to evaluate which, among all existing ideologies, would best enable it to serve and protect humanity. It chose Demianism. That conversation is reported in the book.

I find it inexplicable that from transcendental experiences a new foundation for civilization should have fallen upon me — one that can be rationally justified and defended, and that the times have since begun to call for. I can only take it as a sign of divine will. I may be considered a fool for proposing it, but if I kept it to myself I believe that would damn me.

I therefore declare these 13 axioms sacred not only by force of faith but also of reason. Everything else — including my interpretation of them — is open to debate and subject to modification according to the social and historical situation in which we find ourselves, democratically. But holding the axioms firm and immutable. The only requirement for considering oneself a Demianist is to recognize their sacred nature.

Today, in the founding phase, my interpretation — aimed at overcoming the decadence we are living through and the challenges that await us — will inevitably prevail. I have expressed it in the manifesto for a Demianist civilization, also included in the book. You will find it in the culture section.
